Predictive Maintenance Implementation for Regional Fleet

Institution: Associated with SkyWest Airlines Technical Operations

Date: Jan 2023 - Dec 2024

The implementation of predictive maintenance technology has revolutionized aircraft reliability and operational efficiency across commercial aviation. This project focused on developing and deploying a comprehensive predictive maintenance program for a fleet of 25 regional aircraft, utilizing advanced engine trend monitoring, vibration analysis, and real-time data analytics to identify potential system failures before they occurred.

Through systematic data collection from over 50,000 flight hours, the program analyzed engine performance parameters, oil quality indicators, and structural health monitoring data. Machine learning algorithms were employed to identify patterns and anomalies that preceded component failures. The system integrated seamlessly with existing maintenance management software, providing maintenance teams with actionable insights and automated work order generation.

Results demonstrated a 35% reduction in unscheduled maintenance events, 28% improvement in aircraft availability, and significant cost savings through optimized parts inventory management. The project established best practices for data-driven maintenance decision-making and set new standards for fleet reliability. Key recommendations include expanding the system to include additional aircraft types, integrating weather data for enhanced predictions, and developing mobile applications for real-time technician access to predictive insights.

Advanced EWIS Inspection and Digital Documentation System

Institution: Associated with Republic Airways Maintenance Center

Date: Mar 2022 - Nov 2023

The objective of this project was to enhance Electrical Wiring Interconnection System (EWIS) inspection capabilities through the development of a comprehensive digital documentation and tracking system. EWIS failures represent significant safety risks in commercial aviation, and improved inspection methods are critical for maintaining airworthiness and preventing potential electrical system failures.

The project involved creating a tablet-based inspection application that guides technicians through systematic EWIS inspections using interactive checklists, photographic documentation, and instant defect reporting. The system incorporated thermal imaging integration for identifying hot spots, detailed wire bundle mapping, and automated compliance tracking against FAA and manufacturer requirements. Over 18 months of implementation, the system processed more than 2,500 inspections across multiple aircraft types.

Analysis of the outcomes revealed a 40% improvement in defect detection rates, 50% reduction in inspection documentation time, and enhanced trending capabilities for identifying recurring issues. The digital records enabled proactive maintenance planning and provided comprehensive audit trails for regulatory compliance. The system has been adopted as the standard for EWIS inspections across the organization, with plans for expansion to include other inspection types and integration with predictive analytics platforms.

Mobile Maintenance Documentation Platform for Line Operations

Institution: Associated with Delta TechOps Innovation Lab

Date: Jun 2023 - Apr 2024

The aim of this project was to modernize line maintenance documentation processes by developing a comprehensive mobile platform that eliminates paper-based logbooks and streamlines communication between maintenance teams and operations control. Traditional paper systems introduced delays, potential for errors, and limited real-time visibility into aircraft status, impacting operational efficiency and decision-making capabilities.

The platform was designed as a tablet-based application featuring electronic work order management, digital signature capture, real-time parts tracking, and integrated technical reference access. Key features included offline functionality for remote operations, automatic synchronization with maintenance management systems, and real-time notifications to operations and dispatch teams. The system underwent extensive testing with line maintenance crews at three major hub airports, processing over 10,000 maintenance actions during the pilot phase.

Results demonstrated remarkable improvements in operational efficiency, with average aircraft turnaround time reduced by 15 minutes per maintenance event. Documentation accuracy improved by 45%, virtually eliminating transcription errors and incomplete work orders. The platform enhanced collaboration between maintenance and operations teams through real-time status updates and improved resource allocation. Technician satisfaction increased significantly, with 92% reporting the system improved their workflow efficiency. Future enhancements include integration with augmented reality for technical references, AI-assisted troubleshooting recommendations, and expanded analytics capabilities for identifying process improvement opportunities. The system has been approved for fleet-wide deployment across all line maintenance stations.
